1. CORE PHILOSOPHY
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
Odin is not competing with Google or TripAdvisor.
It is competing with NOISE.

In an era of AI-generated content, fake reviews, and sponsored results,
Odin's value is TRUST — not volume.

Positioning: "Finally, information you can actually trust."
The Knowledge Base number represents the value of trusted data, not just reach.

2. WHAT A USER CAN SEE (VISIBILITY LOGIC)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
A user's visible items come from three sources — NO duplicates:

  1. Their own adds
  2. Their direct friends' adds (set to "Friends" visibility)
  3. Their direct friends' saves (items friends saved from their own circle)

Rule: Same item saved by 3 friends = still counts as 1 item.
Rule: NO friends-of-friends (FoF) exposure. One degree only.
Rule: Items always appear under the original adder's visibility settings.
Rule: The person who ADDS controls visibility. Saves do not change broadcast rules.

WORKED EXAMPLE — CONFIRMED 15 Mar 2026:
  - Stanley is friends with Aden.
  - Aden is friends with Person X. Stanley does NOT know Person X.
  - Person X adds an item and sets visibility to "Friends."
  - Aden sees Person X's item and saves it.
  - Stanley sees the item in his feed because Aden saved it.
  - Stanley sees: full item details (name, description, photo, address).
  - Stanley sees: "Saved by Aden" — the trust signal.
  - Stanley does NOT see: who originally added it. Person X is anonymous to Stanley.

This is called "SAVE INHERITANCE."
Aden's save carries the item one hop further — anonymously.
Stanley trusts Aden. Aden curated it. That is enough signal.
The original adder's identity never travels beyond their intended circle.

THIS IS NOT FRIENDS-OF-FRIENDS (FoF).
The distinction is critical:
  - FoF = you see content from people two hops away, with attribution.
  - Save Inheritance = you see content curated by your direct friend,
    with no attribution to the original source.
  - Trust chain is always exactly ONE degree. Never more.

3. SAVE MECHANICS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
- When a user saves a friend's item, that save is VISIBLE within their circle by default.
- Saves are public within your circle — not broadcast to the world.
- This creates the social proof loop: Add → Friends see it → They save it → You see the save → Feel validated → Add more.
- "Saved by Stanley MAK and Aden Mak" UI pattern = correct. Personal, not just a number.

When adding an item, user chooses visibility:
  - Only me (private)
  - Friends (visible to direct connections)

PRIVACY RULE ON SAVES — CONFIRMED:
- The person who ADDS the item controls visibility.
- A save does NOT change or override the original visibility setting.
- A save carries the item one hop further — but strips the original adder's identity.
- The item's content travels. The source's identity does not.

CONSENT TRANSPARENCY — CONFIRMED DECISION:
- When a user selects "Friends" visibility on the Add page, show this helper text:
  "Your friends can save this, and their circle will see it was saved —
   but not who added it."
- This is the consent moment. One sentence. No fine print.
- This is a FEATURE, not a liability. It tells users exactly how their
  knowledge travels — which is more transparency than any other platform offers.
- See UX file for tooltip implementation details and next session prompt.

4. SOCIAL PROOF — SAVE COUNTS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
- An item may have 100 total saves globally.
- The user only sees saves FROM THEIR CIRCLE.
- If 8 of their friends saved it → show "8 friends saved this."
- Only those 8 matter. Global counts are irrelevant noise.

7. KNOWLEDGE BASE — WHAT COUNTS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
Knowledge Base = total unique items a user can see. Deduped. No double counting.

Counts:
  ✓ Items the user added themselves
  ✓ Items their direct friends added (Friends visibility)
  ✓ Items their direct friends saved (save inheritance, anonymous source)
  ✗ Does NOT count the same item twice even if 3 friends saved it
  ✗ Does NOT count private (Only me) items from friends
  ✗ Does NOT count items from people two hops away with attribution

Why this number matters:
  - It is the most important retention metric in the app.
  - It grows every time a friend adds or saves something new.
  - It makes the value of adding more friends tangible and visible.
  - User should always feel: more connections = bigger Knowledge Base = more value.
  - We are in an age of information overload and fake data.
    Every item in a user's Knowledge Base was put there by a real person they trust.
    That is the core differentiator. Quality over quantity. Signal over noise.

9. USER TESTING FOCUS — 15 Mar 2026
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
When putting this in front of real users, pay close attention to:

  1. Do users understand what "Friends" visibility means after reading the tooltip?
  2. Do users feel comfortable knowing their save can travel one hop anonymously?
  3. Does the tooltip feel reassuring or alarming?
  4. Does anonymous attribution feel fair or frustrating when receiving content?
  5. KEY MOMENT: When a user sees an item with no original source shown —
     do they trust it less, or does "saved by [friend]" provide enough signal?
  6. Does the Knowledge Base number feel motivating?
     Do users want to grow it by adding more friends?
  7. Does the social proof ("8 friends saved this") feel more meaningful
     than a generic review count?
