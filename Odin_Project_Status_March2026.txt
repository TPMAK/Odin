🔄 KEY PIVOTS MADE (AND WHY)
================================================================================

PIVOT 1: Name — "The Community" → ODIN
  Why: "The Community" was too generic and didn't reflect the product's
  identity. Odin conveys knowledge, trust, and intelligence.

PIVOT 2: Family-only → Friend circles
  Why: The trust mechanic doesn't require blood relation — it requires
  genuine personal connection. Opening to friend circles widens TAM
  while preserving the privacy-first model.

PIVOT 3: Platform — Bubble.io → HTML/JS + GitHub Pages
  Why: Faster iteration, lower cost, full control over logic.
  Enabled custom AI search and n8n workflows impossible in Bubble.

PIVOT 4: Model — Generic search → Intent-aware retrieval
  Why: Different queries need different retrieval logic. A recall query
  ("where was that sushi place?") needs different ranking than an
  urgent service query ("I need a dentist now"). Intent routing
  solved relevance without needing more data.

PIVOT 5: Product positioning — "AI search tool" → "Trust layer"
  Why: Competing with Google on search is unwinnable. Competing with
  NOISE (AI-generated content, fake reviews, sponsored results) is
  a real gap. Odin wins on trust, not volume.

Key design decisions:
  • No native app — PWA on web, Siri via iOS Shortcuts
  • No PostGIS — Haversine formula for distance (sufficient for MVP)
  • No manual tags — AI-generated only, not exposed to users
  • n8n handles all AI enrichment (intent, embedding, tags, language)


================================================================================
⚠️ KNOWN RISKS
================================================================================

Technical:
  • Cold start problem — new users see an empty feed until friends connect
  • RLS (Row Level Security) — needs thorough testing as friend graph grows
  • n8n single point of failure — if workflow breaks, captures fail silently

Business:
  • Network effect dependency — value only materialises with 3+ active friends
  • Privacy trust — users may hesitate to add personal data (dentists, private places)
    → Mitigated by: Save Inheritance strips original identity beyond 1 hop.
      Consent tooltip at add-time tells users exactly how far their data travels.
  • Retention — without notifications, users may forget to return

Mitigation in place:
  • Invite flow ensures users see activity from day 1 (no empty start)
  • Visibility controls give users privacy confidence
  • Knowledge Base stat is a retention hook (more friends = bigger number)

🌍 MULTILINGUAL SEARCH & TRANSLATION (Live — March 2026)
================================================================================

Odin supports search in any language. Users type or speak in Japanese, Thai,
French, Chinese, Korean, or any supported language — the AI understands the
intent and returns the most relevant results regardless of what language the
content was originally added in.

Results always return in the original language they were added.
A Translate button on each card lets the user translate "The Word"
(the personal note) into their own language.

  Supported: 13 languages as of March 2026. More easily added.

Design principle:
  Language affects PRESENTATION, not RETRIEVAL.
  Trust and relevance are never reduced because content was added
  in a different language.

Confirmed working (screenshot 15 Mar 2026):
  Japanese query "おいしいコーヒーが飲みたい" → returned relevant Auckland
  coffee results in English, with Chinese and Japanese translation of personal
  note available on the card.

🔒 PRIVACY MODEL — USER-FACING EXPLANATION
================================================================================

(Draft text for in-app FAQ, onboarding, or help screen)

  "Who can see what you add and save?

   You are always in control. When you add something, you choose who sees it:

   • Only me — completely private. Only you can ever see it.
   • Friends — visible to people you've directly connected with on Odin.

   Your friends can never see your private items. And people your friends
   know (but you don't) can never see who added something — even if it
   reaches their feed through a save. Odin only shares identity within
   your direct circle. Never beyond it.

   When you save something a friend added, that save is visible to your
   circle — but it doesn't change who can see the original item. The
   person who added it still controls that.

   Before you post with 'Friends' visibility, we tell you exactly how
   far your item can travel. No surprises.

   No strangers. No algorithms surfacing your data.
   No one outside your circle knows you added it."
